# Episode 22. Grain Left Behind

When the three stepped onto the external platform, the structure was more coldly and thinly alive than expected.

It wasn't a full vacuum section. Between the severed outer wall and the half-collapsed corridor, an old containment field had refused to die, sustaining a cold, thin but definitely breathable layer of air. The smell of long-dead power lines, the cold dust that only comes from severed metal surfaces, and a very faint remnant of ozone. The smell of someone having killed the function here—and still not having killed it completely.

Behind them, Han Jiwoo's ship hung half-wedged against the structure's outer wall, at an angle that was neither fully docked nor withdrawn. Docking flush would be faster, but just as irreversible. Right now, leaving room for error mattered more than speed.

Seorin's voice came through the short-range closed channel first.

"Looks even worse from out here."

Han Jiwoo answered briefly.

"Still not dead though."

Sion, instead of replying, looked into the structure's interior. After clearing the first discrimination, the guidance afterglow that had stirred to life deep inside kept appearing and then sinking back as if dead. Not a steady guidance line. More like it was testing whoever was reading—follow and it cuts once more; stop and it comes back just a little.

Ater, watching the flow, said quietly.

"It's not a continuously readable signal. It's a structure that pulls you in while checking your responses."

Sern added immediately.

"It guides the visitor inward while making them lose their way the moment they get a single step of the sequence wrong."

This place was still a structure that cut wrong hands. One wrong touch and the reaction would die before the fragment, and with it, the reason for coming this far.

Sion pressed the structure's surface once with his toe, then moved slowly inward. The gaps between metal panels were far from smooth. Cut marks, patched marks, and traces of old alignment lines tangled together. Not a path you could walk like an official dock, but one that only continued inward when you stepped along the remaining grain.

Sern said from behind, his voice low.

"Third panel on the left—load is unstable."

Sion shifted his foot immediately.

Ater, at the rear, was scanning the markings left on the structure's walls. At first they looked like plain cut marks, but up close there was pattern. The cuts that had killed the core guidance line and the marks that had deliberately preserved the secondary alignment sat on different layers. The hand that damaged and the hand that preserved might not have been the same.

He stopped and said very quietly.

"Sion. This wasn't cut in a single pass."

Sion's stride stopped.

"You mean it was touched more than once?"

"Yes. First it was cut to kill the function, then someone preserved a minimum discrimination unit and alignment section."

Sern picked up the thread at once.

"Then what remains now is not debris from the original, but a residual structure someone intentionally kept alive."

Sion heard that and smiled very slowly.

"Then it's even more certain."

Just from the slot and fragments visible before them, it was clear: what someone had erased was not a single name, but the path that name was supposed to reach.

At that moment, a very faint light flickered from a panel groove near his feet.

Sion dropped low immediately. There was a thin, long groove, and along its edge were specification marks where an insert-type record fragment would once have fitted.

"Here."

Sern came to his side at once.

"An insertion slot?"

"Probably."
Sion said, running his fingertip along the groove.
"But it's empty now."

Ater moved closer and examined the marks.

"This isn't a place where a fragment was tested for fit. It's a place where the type of fragment was verified."

Sion slowly raised his head.

"Then what we're carrying isn't just a record fragment either."

Seorin called from the hull side.

"Don't you dare tell me you're about to try inserting it."

"Not inserting."
Sion cut.
"This is a structure that reads first, then compares."

Sern, examining below the groove, said quietly.

"Wait."

All eyes converged on that spot.

Just below the slot, wedged in a gap between severed metal panels, was a very thin fragment. Buried under dust and ash, it looked like a metal shard at first, but when Sern very carefully picked it up with gloved fingertips, a fine grain appeared on its surface.

A record fragment.

Not a complete panel. Thumbnail-sized, charred edges, surface half-dead. But one side clearly bore artificial lines. Too short for a sentence, too severed for coordinates.

Sion drew a very shallow breath.

"There it is."

Han Jiwoo asked from the hull side immediately.

"What."

Sion answered without taking his eyes off the fragment.

"The first real piece."

Ater looked down at the fragment in Sern's hand. Small, incomplete, explaining nothing yet. And still everyone knew. That single small fragment was enough to prove that the path that had brought them here was no phantom.

He said very quietly.

"This isn't a record fragment. It's the first physical evidence that the erased path actually existed."

But Sion was looking longer at the slot's edge than at the fragment itself.

Seorin noticed first.

"Why."

Instead of answering, Sion touched the inner edge of the slot with his fingertip. The inside was empty, but the way it was empty was wrong. Not a groove long vacant, but a spot where a much larger panel had been fitted until very recently and then pulled out in haste. The charred powder hadn't settled evenly, and inside the metal groove, a freshly scraped line was still visible.

Sern read the same grain at once.

"There was a larger panel."

Ater added very quietly.

"And it was removed recently."

A short silence.

Seorin narrowed her brow.

"So we didn't find a fragment. We picked up the crumbs left behind after someone cleaned the place out."

Sion nodded slowly.

"Yeah. What was originally inside was much bigger. This is probably the piece that snapped off when they pulled it out in a hurry."

Han Jiwoo swallowed a curse from the cockpit.

"That's the thing I hate hearing most."

Sion pointed to the outer metal plate below the slot.

A trace across the dust. Not a footprint from someone entering the structure, but closer to the skid of someone clutching something heavy to their body and turning back out in haste. Not a complete footprint, but a mark where someone had lost their center of gravity once and caught themselves.

Sern immediately dropped to a knee.

"Recent."

"How recent."
Seorin asked.

Sern quickly scanned the trace, the dust, and the broken powder layer.

"Not very long ago. High likelihood they exited before we cleared the first discrimination."

Ater's gaze moved to the deeper darkness inside the structure.

"Then they may not have gotten far."

Sion looked at the small fragment in his hand, then back at the empty slot. The sensation of having obtained the first piece was real. But just as real was the fact that something far more important had been taken first by someone who had already fled.

He said very quietly.

"Now we finally have a real trail."

Han Jiwoo asked from the hull side.

"So. Do we chase right now?"

Sion didn't answer immediately.

Sern was calculating the recent traces, Ater was turning over the fact that whoever had extracted the larger fragment could read this structure, and Seorin was watching all three faces in turn, gauging who would tip first toward the reckless option.

Deep inside the structure, a very faint guidance reaction stirred to life once more. A signal that there was still more inside—and that the end of the path someone had rushed through and severed was not completely dead.

Sion slipped the small fragment into his recovery pocket and said quietly.

"We secure the first piece. And whoever took the larger one first hasn't gotten very far."

Ater continued as if picking up the thread.

"Then the choice is one of two. Check deeper inside first, or bite the trail now."

And before the five of them, what was opening was not a simple ruin survey but the real next stage—chasing who had read this path first and who had fled with the larger fragment in hand.
