# Episode 69. The Air of Staying Before Leaving

Even after Harun's settlement ended, the scrap market did not immediately return to its old face.

But it had not become an entirely different one either.

Seorin found that ambiguous gap strangely unsettling.

The flipped board had been sorted. Zahir had redrawn his lines. Harun had been punished.

So everyone should have quickly resumed talking, haggling, arguing about what values went up or down tonight—acting like the usual market.

But the scrap market did not pretend nothing had happened that fast.

People moved in lower voices. Feet passing between tent covers were one beat slower than usual. Some murmured while watching the return lines. Some swept past the pile of metal plates torn from the arena's edge.

Everyone was going back to living, but their bodies still remembered what had just happened.

Kael watched and said shortly.

"They bounce back fast, though."

Ater answered low.

"They have to survive."

Luhai muttered right beside them.

"It's kind of cool, though."

Seorin shot him a look.

"What is."

"No, just."
Luhai shrugged.
"Everything got flipped this hard, but nobody actually looks like it's all over."

Sern said very low.

"If they collapsed, there'd be no value left to roll."

The words were dry, which made them sound all the more like Hazran.

Seorin cast her gaze past the railing.

The work inside still showed no sign of ending. What Sion, Jiwoo, and Aka were seeing could not be known. But now, not knowing did not immediately bring unease. What was larger was the sense that they were touching something too important to disturb right now.

Meanwhile, the outside was coming back to life on its own terms.

Someone righted an overturned tent cover. Someone patched a torn heat-blocking cloth. Someone passed carrying water jugs. Desert skiff wreckage from the arena outskirts was being dragged out one piece at a time to other compartments.

Harun had truly begun the restoration with his own hands.

That sight seemed to shut the scrap market people's mouths even more than otherwise.

More than a scene of apology in words, the scene of a hand that should not have had to—lifting what it had broken with its own strength—was a larger sentence in this place.

Kael watched from a distance and said.

"He's not running."

Sern answered.

"Running would be admitting he was wrong."

"Then not running?"
Luhai asked.

Sern looked toward Harun for a moment before speaking.

"Means he still thinks he was right, in his own way."

Seorin felt that oddly grating yet not incorrect.

Harun was detestable. But not flat. That was what made him a more exhausting person.

Zahir had returned to his seat at some point.

His face looked as though punishing a man in the courtyard just now, redrawing the board's entire lines—none of it was anything particularly special for this place.

But Seorin knew now. That man was not indifferent. He was the kind who kept turning the order back with an indifferent face.

Ater seemed to feel something similar.

He said low.

"There is a reason this place has lasted longer than expected."

Luhai laughed and caught it.

"Admitting it only now?"

Ater did not respond to that.

Instead, he looked at the scrap market floor below the railing and added.

"There is an order maintained to look like disorder."

Luhai clicked his tongue.

"You talk so complicated."

Kael summed it up shortly.

"He means there's a rule that pretends to be chaos."

Luhai nodded immediately at that.

"That I understand."

Seorin nearly smirked listening to the three of them.

Whatever the case, only one thing mattered right now.

The mood was not one of fleeing in a rush.

That sense was larger than expected. From the start, Hazran had looked like a place to pass through briefly. But now it was starting to look like a place to catch a breath before departure, make repairs, stock what was needed, and look at people's faces one more time.

Luhai was the first to voice that atmosphere.

"We're not leaving right away, are we?"
He said.
"Don't even know how long the stuff inside will take, the arena's wrecked like that, and the market'll need days to get rolling again."

Seorin cut in immediately.

"Since when were you part of 'we.'"

Luhai did not even blink.

"I mean, the vibe, right?"
He said.
"Feels kind of wrong to just leave me behind at this point."

Kael laughed shortly hearing that.

"Why."

"No reason, just."
Luhai feigned nonchalance.
"Seems like there's a lot worth watching."

Seorin cut again.

"Shamelessness is a talent."

Luhai did not deny it at all.

"Yeah."
He said.
"This place is too good to leave after just one day."

Normally Seorin would have scoffed. But strangely, this time no retort came right away.

Too good to leave.

That was more right than expected.

The scrap market was still restoring. Inside, Sion and Jiwoo were bridging something with Aka. Harun was serving his punishment. Zahir was rolling Hazran again.

The time that formed in between felt less like dead space and more like time for looking, just once, at the faces that appeared while staying in this place.

Ater asked very quietly.

"Then it would be wise to organize what we need while we wait."

Kael answered at once.

"Parts, fuel, water, heat-blocking cloth. We'll add more once we see the hull situation."

Sern added low.

"And people too."

Seorin looked at him.

Sern was calm as always.

"Who sees what here, who takes what away, who remembers whom and how."
He said.
"That'll get sorted too."

Those words lingered strangely long.

Seorin looked below the railing again without a word.

The arena being restored. Tent covers being raised again. The market coming slowly alive. And the people who had not yet come out from inside.

Leaving was not yet.

Instead, an air that said they would stay properly in this place at least once before departing was settling in, little by little.
