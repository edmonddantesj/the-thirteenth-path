# Episode 93. The Heavier One That Arrived Next

When Sern said *something large is coming*,
Sion first assumed it simply meant more numbers.

It didn't.

What came this time differed not in count
but in weight and purpose.

The metallic sound from beyond the right outer corridor
was clearly different from the android lines that had been pushing into the market.
The rhythm was slower.
But each time it rang, it pressed down through the floor itself.

Harun noticed immediately.

For the first time, he glanced back—very briefly.

"Sern."

Sern answered, low.

"Yes. Not a front-line unit."

Ater continued.

"It is a breacher."

That required no long explanation.

The lines that had been entering until now
were the kind that made gaps,
read direction,
and tore through one section at a time.

But if a breacher enters,
it means crushing that entire calculus with force.

The air inside Hazran
sank one more time at that single word.

Even Sion, bracing the second group's stretcher,
felt as if the weight on his hands had suddenly grown heavier.

They had barely held until now.
And now the method of holding itself might break.

Jiwoo looked up from the hull wall immediately.

"If that hits here, it's over."
She said, low.

Aka had been listening outward
and stopped breathing for the briefest instant.

It was terrible.
But strangely,
it also felt heartbreaking—unbearably sad.

Aka couldn't hold onto the reason before it slipped away.
She murmured, very brief.

"It pushes flat."

Sion didn't understand the meaning right away.
But Ater's face showed immediate comprehension.

"It will be a pressing format."
He said.
"Rather than cutting—ramming, collapsing, flattening the path, then passing the rear through."

Harun swallowed a short breath.

That was worse.

What Hazran was doing now
was defense that used narrow corridors and turning angles,
low sandbags and wreckage,
to kill speed and control direction.

But if the enemy simply pushes that structure flat,
all the thin layers of time they had built could collapse at once.

Harun didn't think further.

"Keep loading the second group."
He said, low.
"Stop and we all die here together."

Cruel words.
But right.

Zahir's face said he had reached the same conclusion.

He looked once at the hull,
then swept across Nasim, Seorin, Luhai, and Sion in turn.

"Attach the second group to the end."
He said.
"No stopping before then."

When the words fell,
the hands inside Hazran moved again.

Nasim shouted in his nearly ruined voice.

"Stretchers first! Fast—but don't push!"

Seorin directly pushed shoulders at the front of the second line, aligning angles.
Luhai rewrapped the cloth that kept slipping from a wounded person's hand.
Sion tamped the sand beneath the hull ramp with his feet to keep it from shifting,
using his body to keep people's paths from tangling.

That moment,
the thing finally revealed itself at the end of the right outer corridor.

The first impression
was that even the word *humanoid* didn't fit.

Low.
Thick.
Its forward center of gravity strangely heavy.
If the androids seen until now had been *an unbreaking line*,
this was the *crushing head* placed at the front of that line.

The joints were thicker.
The forward plating was built to open paths even at the cost of absorbing cuts and disruptions.
The place that should have looked like a face was even less a face than before.
Just a black surface built for collision and pressure alone.

Luhai drew in a breath without a word the moment he saw it.

Seorin, for the first time, went silent instead of cursing.

Harun spoke low.

"I take that one."

Sern raised his head immediately.

"Not alone."

"I know."
Harun cut him short.
"But someone has to stand in front to bend it."

Ater added, very quick.

"Do not engage head-on. Look at the left knee and lower coupling line first. The upper body is built to be discarded."

Those words resembled what they'd learned about the other androids,
but sounded more frightening.

Because if a unit was built to enter even at the cost of discarding its upper body,
the side blocking it could take far worse damage.

Sern gripped the cutting tool again, pressing his side wound.

Sion could see he was reaching his limit,
and also knew he couldn't stop him right now.

The second group's first stretcher went up the ramp.
Jiwoo immediately checked the coupling's tremor with her hand.
Aka, eyes still closed, was measuring the next weight.

But through all of it,
Sion's gaze kept drifting to that *heavier thing* outside.

If that pushes in once,
this place could truly end.

Harun lowered his stance.
Sern spread to the opposite side.
Ater read angles from behind in the shortest breaths.

And the black breacher unit
put its weight forward for the first time.

That one step alone
made a low grinding sound through the wreckage laid beneath the corridor.

The time Hazran had been buying until now
was about to collide with something one weight heavier.
