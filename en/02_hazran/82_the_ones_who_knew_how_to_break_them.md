# Episode 82. The Ones Who Knew How to Break Them

After the first front line was built inside the market,
Hazran could no longer think of outside and inside as separate.

One section falls and the next becomes the battlefield.
One corridor is emptied and another one jams.

Inside it all,
Sion realized the most frightening thing wasn't the androids themselves.

It was facing them without knowing how to break them.
That was worse.

And right now,
the people who knew that answer best were Ater and Sern.

At the end of the first corridor where two black units had fallen,
the third and fourth were already pushing in.

The front unit climbed right over the fallen body.
The rear unit followed at precisely the same angle.
Block one side and what comes isn't the other side—
it's the next layer at the same spot.

Harun clenched his jaw watching that flow.

"Straight on won't cut it."
He said, low.

Sern answered immediately.

"Correct."

He was already crouched low, nearly at the center of the battlefield,
reading the android joints and response speed.

"Not the knee or neck."
Sern said, brief.
"They discard those. The priority coupling axis has to be severed."

One of the Hazran shooters asked urgently.

"Where."

Sern spoke without taking his eyes off.

"Inside the lower right rib line."

The shooter's expression locked for a moment.
An odd location if you thought in terms of a human body.
But Ater took the words immediately.

"It mimics the human thorax."
He said, cold.
"Built to look like something worth protecting. In reality, the priority switching axis is placed inside."

The words gave Sion a stranger kind of chill.

Because it meant the parts made to look human
were fake structures designed to deceive humans.

Harun didn't ask more.

"Shoot there."
He cut it short.

Two shooters popped from cover and pulled triggers simultaneously.

The first shot missed.
The android twisted its body in the briefest instant.
But the second hit.
The inner right line of the black body caved for a moment,
and this time the movement clearly broke in an abnormal way.

Sern drove in almost simultaneously.

He didn't go head-on.
He grazed under a fallen sandbag as if stepping over it,
entered the blind angle where the android's vision bent once. Then he drew the short cutting tool in his right hand upward, severing the coupling axis that had just been shaken—for the second time.

One black unit
truly stopped this time.

The eyes of the Hazran people who saw it changed for an instant.

It can be broken.

Not completely,
but there was definitely a place in that line where they died.

But Ater didn't let even that faint relief linger.

"The same spot will not work twice."
He said, low.
"They switch patterns immediately."

The instant those words fell,
the two rear units changed their advance angle.
Until now they had been eating straight through the narrow corridor.
This time they moved to cut the left barrier first—trying to widen their field of vision.

Ater called out immediately.

"Do not abandon the left. If that opens, it is a straight line to the next corridor."

Harun snapped his hand.

"Hold the left!"

Two people running in from Nasim's side shoved another metal barrier forward,
and a thin coupling plate Kael had passed along was forced into the gap.
That instant an android cutting beam flew in and split half the metal plate.
But it didn't open completely.

Exactly that one beat.

Ater didn't miss the gap.

"Sern."

One name.

That was enough.

Sern had already slid into the shadow on the opposite side.
This time he went under the second unit, not the first. He feinted low as if engaging the front, then drove the cutting tool deep inside the rear leg's coupling line and pulled out sideways.

When the second unit's leg buckled to one side,
the third unit pushing from behind caught on it fully for the first time.

Harun shouted that instant.

"Push now, all together!"

Three Hazran shooters fired at once.
Two short concussion plates detonated in sequence.
The two fallen units half-blocked the corridor,
and metal dust and torn tent scraps tangled and crashed over them.

The first front line inside the market
had bound that wave for a real beat, for the first time.

Luhai watched the scene and muttered with an almost disbelieving face.

"It works…"

Nasim heard that and swallowed a low breath.

"We have to make it work."
He said.

But in that voice there was less relief
and more the kind of tension that comes from having just found one method.

Sion felt the same thing.

Without Ater and Sern,
Hazran would still be getting pushed back without knowing where to hit to make those things stop.

The two were clearly people who came from the Empire.
But on this battlefield, right now,
that was exactly why they were the hands most needed.

And that fact
made the war taste more bitter.
