# Episode 78. The First Line That Moved Without Breath

That war has arrived at your doorstep
usually doesn't look like a grand explosion at first.

It's the opposite.

One thing, very far away,
shows itself first in a way that is too regular—and therefore wrong.

Above Hazran's outer waterway,
in the center of a dawn where the heat layer hadn't fully woken,
Sern was the first to pin the wrongness down.

"Visible."
He said, low.

At that single word,
the air around them tightened together.

Sion looked toward the outer edge immediately.

At first he thought it was nothing.
A single black line at most.
A thin shadow trembling slightly above the heat.

But soon he could tell.

It wasn't trembling.
It was the kind that kept its angle even inside trembling air.

Not human.

Humans don't move like that at that distance.
Walk long enough on sand and your stride falls out of step.
When the heat layer is alive, even silhouettes get pushed along with it.
But that black line was different. When one line wavered, the line behind it came alive at exactly the same interval. Not falling—replacing.

Ater said, very low.

"Confirmed."

Sion thought he understood why those two syllables sounded so cold.

Confirmation.

No longer a sign.
It had substance now.

"How many."
Zahir asked.

Harun was already watching from the edge of the outer perimeter.

"Only the first line is visible."
He answered.
"But that isn't just the front coming."

Nasim swallowed a curse under his breath.

Sion looked outside again.

Now he could see.
Not one black line,
but other lines layered behind it.
Still too far to count,
but the method was already visible.

It didn't break.

When the front line was pushed, the rear line filled the same interval.
No stops.
No hesitation.
No gaps inside the flow.

Aka murmured, very low.

"No breath."

Those words were more precise than anyone else's.

That wasn't an army charging.
That was a line without the rhythm of living things pausing briefly out of fear.

Machine.

The air outside Hazran understood that name together.

Someone on the return line clenched a fist without realizing it. The people deeper in the market grew quieter. Nothing had physically touched them yet, but the entire city tensed as if refusing to match its breathing to that line.

Harun spoke briefly.

"Prepare first disruption on the outer perimeter."

The moment the order dropped,
the thin metal plates Hazran had pre-positioned outside all tilted at once. Small heat-deflection plates hidden under the sand activated, beginning to warp the outer line of sight. The frame wreckage torn from the arena was laid out now less like shields and more like teeth to break the enemy's straight lines.

The Empire's first line did not slow down.

That was the more frightening part.

Humans would stop at least once when an unfamiliar disruption appeared.
Re-read the angle.
Check whether front and rear lines still matched.
Watch for where the traps might be.

But that line had none of that.

When the first three shifted slightly out of alignment,
the two behind immediately filled the empty angle.
Lateral spacing held.
Instead of stopping, the calculation seemed to get faster.

Sern spoke low.

"The front line is the expendable line."

Ater took it immediately.

"Most likely."

Sion felt more tense because those two voices were so calm.

It was because they knew.
How abnormal that was—
both of them saw it more clearly than anyone.

Harun signaled the outer perimeter personnel.

"First contact won't last long."
He said, low.
"Don't try to stop them. Bend them."

When those words ended,
two desert skiffs slid low beneath the outer perimeter line.
Not for racing.
Pure Hazran-style improvised disruption craft. They kicked up sand and metal dust deliberately wide, warping the first line's vision once more, then slipped back into the inner shadows.

One android in the first line adjusted direction for the briefest instant.

And immediately,
two units behind filled the vacated angle.

Where a human would have wavered,
that side simply replaced.

Luhai watched it and swallowed a low breath.

"Damn…"

Inside that short exclamation there was more disgust than surprise.

Sion clenched his fist without realizing.

That was frightening.
Not frightening because it was strong.
Frightening because it did not hesitate.

Jiwoo's expression hardened the moment she saw that line.

Sion looked at her face and understood immediately.
This was not a simple enemy.
To Jiwoo, that line
was the result of using machines in the worst possible way.

Jiwoo spoke, very low.

"If they're going to run them like that…"

The sentence cut there.
But it didn't need to finish.

She was angry.
Not the wariness of facing weapons or troops.
Anger at the world itself for making machines this way.

Then,
one of the outer metal plates split in half with a sharp cutting sound.

Direct contact was still far away.
But the moment something inside that line aligned once,
the thin defensive barrier Hazran had raised was severed with too much precision.

Ater spoke immediately.

"Cutting class."

Sern added right away.

"The type that pins and severs."

Harun heard the analysis and didn't look back.
Instead he spoke low.

"Good."
He said.
"Then we make them walk longer."

Zahir was looking in the same direction.

He still did not raise his voice.
But the entire market was already moving inside that silence.

Hazran had seen the first line.

Moving without breath.
Filling vacated positions immediately.
A line that resembled nothing of human hesitation.

And that line
was now actually walking toward Hazran.
