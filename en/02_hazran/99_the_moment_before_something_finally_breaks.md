# Episode 99. The Moment Before Something Finally Breaks

The speed was already near its limit.

After Zahir's order to prepare the fourth group,
the inside of Hazran got faster,
and that was precisely why everything grew more unstable.

Sion knew before seeing it now.

A line that's gotten too fast sounds different when it shakes.
The rhythm of people's feet on sand falls out of sync.
The sound of stretcher legs scraping the metal ramp's edge turns rougher.
Someone's breath-holds get shorter.

That meant
one wrong step by one person could bring the whole thing down together.

At the front of the fourth line,
something like that nearly happened.

A man bracing a wounded person
mistepped because the person ahead crowded too fast.
The stretcher end tilted once.
Someone following behind reached out reflexively,
and the entire line nearly lurched sideways.

Sion threw himself toward it.

"Stop!"
He shouted.
"Stop right now!"

Nasim screamed almost at the same time.

"One section at a time!"

Seorin braced the stretcher from below with her foot,
pressing the nearly toppling body against the hull-side railing.
Luhai pushed the person behind with both palms to the chest,
preventing them from leaning any further forward.

Barely once,
a truly large sway was bent back.

But in those few seconds,
the right front line had thinned again.

Harun was barely using his bleeding arm at all now.
Sern's movement was still short and precise, but clearly more winded.
Ater had cut his words down further—
not because there was more room,
but because the board had narrowed to where even the gaps for speaking had to be saved.

"Above."
"Now."
"Back."

Three words were enough.

But if those three came late,
someone dies here immediately.

The left corridor was the same.

Kael was holding with one hastily reassigned hand
for an almost unbelievable stretch,
but one of the two low barriers he'd raised was already half-dead.
When the metal plate rang hard once more,
Kael exhaled short.

"Can't take the next one."

The moment Sion heard that,
his heart sank harder.

The right: Harun holding it while bleeding.
The left: Kael saying he's at the limit.
And the line before the hull:
can't stop.

Zahir still barely changed his expression.

To Sion's eyes now,
he looked less cold and more like someone watching to the very end.

Where will break first.
Who to send out next.
And after that, who must be left.

A face already thinking those things.

Just seeing that face
chilled Sion's chest.

Jiwoo, at the hull entrance, right before receiving the fourth group,
truly clenched her jaw this time.

"Any more collapse and I can't take them."
She said, low.

Aka, beside her, listening to the outside and the hull's trembling together,
spoke very quietly.

"They are still coming."

That wasn't comfort.
It was just fact.

Still coming.
The ship,
the people,
the time.

But the moment the word *still* was attached,
it also meant it wouldn't be strange if it stopped at any moment.

While the fourth group's first three were being readied,
Nasim sorted people again in his hoarse voice.

"You, back."
"You, now."
"No—crying slows it down. Cry while you walk."

Luhai didn't even answer.
He just gripped a child's hand and a wounded person's coat hem at the same time, holding the line.
Seorin's hands moved before her eyes now.
Sion was already pushing his body in before the line could sway, right beside her.

Everyone was gradually
beginning to move less like people and more like functions.

That was the most frightening part.

And right then,
a sound different from anything before came from the right corridor.

Not a sharp cutting sound.
Not a heavy breaching sound.

The low, grinding fracture sound
of something that had held for a long time
finally being twisted until it broke.

Ater's face tightened—very faintly, for the first time.

"Harun!"

Sern turned his body almost simultaneously.

In that moment,
Sion could tell before he even saw what it was.

Now, truly,
something was about to break.

A person.
A line.
A path.
Or a heart.

He didn't know yet.

But Hazran
had clearly arrived at the moment right before.
