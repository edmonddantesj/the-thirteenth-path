# Episode 103. Each Small Restart Cost More

The moment a line shakes once
and barely starts moving again.

Each short restart
began consuming a far greater price than before.

Sion felt it more and more sharply.

Before, if someone nearly fell,
someone caught them,
the line reconnected,
and that counted as one beat bought.

Not anymore.

Now, inside Hazran,
to create one small restart,
someone has to run more,
someone has to hold longer,
someone has to bleed more.

The single motion just now—Seorin pushing a slow person's waist to reconnect the line—
was only possible because someone behind was holding the front line longer.

Behind every small restart,
a larger price was already laid.

Around the midpoint of the fourth group,
Sern's breathing grew visibly rougher in the right corridor.

He still moved short and precise,
but now each time he drove in and pulled out,
the blood spreading from his side wound was more visible.

Ater saw it and said, very brief.

"Sern, slow by half a beat."

Sern answered, low.

"Slow down and it opens."

That answer came so instantly
that Sion's chest felt heavier.

No time to think.
The body knows first.
Save half a beat here and his body breaks less.
Slow half a beat and another line opens.

Harun had almost stopped speaking entirely.

He held the tilted breacher as an obstacle,
buying time by deflecting, bending, twisting instead of head-on collision. His left arm was nearly dead. That meant he had to use his whole body more.

Which was more dangerous.
When one part dies,
the rest wears down faster to compensate.

The left corridor was deteriorating similarly.

One of the low metal barriers Kael had raised
started tearing from its end edge this time.
He immediately pressed it down with his foot, bracing it, and spoke brief to the person behind.

"When this goes, close the second barrier immediately."

"That blocks the rear corridor too."

"I know."
Kael cut him off.
"Close it first anyway."

The moment Sion heard that exchange,
he realized the left had truly reached the stage of *shutting things before they collapse*.

Hazran was now
counting the spaces to abandon faster than the spaces it could hold.

Before the hull, Nasim split the line once more.

"When the fourth group ends, fifth group attaches immediately!"
He shouted in his hoarse voice.
"Don't leave a gap in between!"

Sion nearly closed his eyes at those words.

It meant truly leaving no breathing room between groups now.

But Zahir didn't stop it.
Which meant
that judgment was correct.

Luhai was already pulling the front people of the fifth group into position.
Seorin watched the fourth group's last three clear the ramp,
while simultaneously cutting with her hands so the next three didn't crowd too close.
Sion, between the two,
felt less like he was holding people and more like he was holding a current.

People's faces were all starting to look the same now.

Afraid faces.
Near-crying faces.
Clenched faces.
Surrendered faces.
All mixed together,
but strangely, the movements were converging.

Everyone was eventually
becoming a body trying to survive inside this speed and this spacing.

Jiwoo read the fourth group's final trembling from the hull entrance
and clenched her jaw, very brief.

"The next one will be rougher."
She said, low.

Aka asked.

"The ship?"

"The people too."
Jiwoo answered.

That was exactly right.

By now, the way the hull shook
and the way the people shook were too alike.
Both near their limits.
Both still holding.
Both could break at any moment without surprise.

Zahir, in the center of it all,
looked like a man with only the final calculation left.

He said, brief.

"Pull the fifth group's front three."

That moment,
Sern swayed hard in the right corridor.

He didn't fall outright.
But a foot stepping on wreckage buckled deep,
and his body leaned sideways harder than expected.

Ater reached out almost simultaneously and pushed him clear.
Then he directly received and deflected an upper angle coming toward his own side.

A short grinding sound.

The end of Ater's sleeve tore in a long line.

Not a deep cut.
But now that side too—
as much as Sern had wavered,
Ater had begun wearing down more directly.

Sion clenched his teeth watching that scene.

To create one small restart,
more and more people were being shaved away together now.

The fourth group's last three crossed into the hull.
And almost simultaneously,
the fifth group's first three were pulled forward.

The line held.

That meant being alive.
But it also meant
the price of keeping that line was growing.

Hazran was now,
for each small restart that kept things moving one more time,
burning more and more blood and strength and people.
