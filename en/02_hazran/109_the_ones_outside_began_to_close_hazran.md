# Episode 109. The Ones Outside Began to Close Hazran

The moment Sion stepped inside the hull,
the first sensation wasn't relief.

It was a suffocating sense of loss.

He had come inside,
yet part of his body felt like it was still outside.

Seorin said nothing.
Luhai leaned against the wall and couldn't raise his head.
Kael stood just past the ramp,
still looking out.

The inside of the hull was narrow, rough, unstable.
But harder to endure than that
was the fact that now the people left outside had to be watched from in here.

Zahir.
Nasim.
Harun.
Nahira.
And the hands that had to hold Hazran's outside to the very last.

They were no longer *people holding together*.
They were the people left outside.

That fact became real so fast
that even after entering the hull, Sion kept feeling the compulsion to go back out.

But Zahir's words were still inside his ears.

*Carry what happened here on your backs and go.*

Not that staying repays.
That leaving is the carrying.

He still couldn't fully accept it,
which made it heavier.

Jiwoo was checking the hull's condition from just inside the entrance, almost without pause.

"Hold the braces."
"Clear the inside more."
"Don't block right in front of the entrance."

Her voice was still sharp.
But Sion could hear the trembling mixed inside.

Jiwoo knew too.
Every movement made from inside from now on
was only possible because the people outside were buying the time.

Seorin spoke low.

"And coming in like this, that's the end?"

That was less a question
and closer to a sneer aimed at herself.

"When we're the ones who started this war."

No one could answer right away.

That sentence pierced the air inside the hull precisely.

Because we came,
Hazran turned from haven to battlefield.
Then is breathing in here really right?

Luhai, leaning against the wall, said very low.

"But Zahir told us to go."

Even that
sounded closer to persuading himself.

Luhai's eyes kept drifting outside.
He was a person of Hazran.
Watching that city close from inside
tears a person apart in a different way from the guilt Sion's group felt.

Nahira was still outside.

Aka, from just inside the entrance,
watched that direction without a word.

The quietest person inside the hull right now was Aka,
and that was precisely why Sion found the silence harder to bear.

Nahira had sent Aka off.
But stayed herself.
That was a farewell far more definitive than any word.

Outside, Nasim was shouting in his hoarse voice.

"Pull the barriers!"
"Shrink the inside!"
"Clear that space!"

Those sounds were no longer organizing to save people.
They were the sounds of folding and closing Hazran itself.

Kael muttered from inside the entrance.

"The left is fully dead now."

He said what he saw, exactly as it was.
No lies, no comfort.

"That won't open again."

When those words fell,
Sion felt viscerally that the city called Hazran was truly losing functions one by one,
leaving behind only its final order.

Harun was leaning behind the inner defensive line, far back,
still looking toward the corridor.

His body had already broken down considerably.
But his gaze hadn't broken to the very end.
Even if he couldn't stand at the front himself,
just remaining in that spot
seemed to show that Hazran wasn't over yet.

Sern and Ater hadn't fully come inside yet.

They were closer to the side still holding the last of the outside,
needing to grip time just a little longer.
But they knew now too.
This wasn't a front line to hold for long.
It was a front line that only lasted until the closing hands found their places.

Sion stood just inside the entrance
and looked down at his own hands.

Hands that had been holding people,
bracing metal,
catching falling bodies until just now.
But right now, they could do nothing.

That helplessness
choked him more rawly than even the debt.

We brought the war here.
But the ones closing are those people.

Isn't this structure too cowardly?

The moment Sion unconsciously stepped forward,
Jiwoo caught his arm immediately.

"Don't."
She said, low.
"If you go back out, everything collapses."

Sion clenched his jaw.

"But they're the only ones left out there."

Jiwoo couldn't answer right away either.

That brief silence
was more honest.

Yes.
They're the only ones left.
And that's exactly why, now more than ever,
the ones inside must hold.
The ship has to stay alive
so that the choice Hazran forced through doesn't go to waste.

Aka spoke for the first time then, very low.

"Watch."

Everyone looked her way.

Aka was still looking outside.

"Watch to the end."

Short words.
But Sion thought he understood what they meant.

Don't live pretending not to know.
Don't leave as if fleeing.
How Hazran closes.
Who stays and bears what.
Watch it all with your own eyes, to the very end.

Outside the hull,
Zahir looked inside one last time.

Sion felt that gaze reach them.

Eyes confirming.
Whether they had truly boarded.
Whether they were truly ready to leave.
Whether it was truly his turn to close now.

And then Zahir slowly turned away.

That moment,
Sion understood clearly for the first time.

The people left outside
had truly begun to close Hazran.
