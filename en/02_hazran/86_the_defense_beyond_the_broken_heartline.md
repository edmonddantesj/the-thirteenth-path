# Episode 86. The Defense Beyond the Broken Heartline

After the central market line closed,
Hazran looked, for a moment, as if it had truly stopped breathing.

The sound of everything collapsing had been loud,
but the silence that followed was strangely louder.

Sion found that short silence frightening.

Silence born in the middle of war
isn't the end—it's closer to the time when the next collision settles into place.
He felt he understood that with his body now.

And truly,
that time was not long.

Beyond the collapsed central line,
a low metallic sound rang out as a line that had lost direction found its angle again.

Harun spoke immediately.

"They're coming around."

At that single line,
the air inside Hazran moved again.

The gap made by abandoning half the market had clearly bought time.
But time was all it bought.
The enemy hadn't vanished.
Now, unable to enter wide,
the wave would seek narrower, deeper paths to come through.

Zahir looked at the ruined central line a moment longer,
then turned his gaze.

"Rebind with three inner lines."
He said, low.
"Don't block wide. Break it small."

Harun took it immediately.

"First line feeds the corridor, second line bends them, third line guards the people."

Short words.
But by now, that was enough for Hazran's people.

The battlefield had changed completely.

Until just now, the market had folded around one central line.
Now it was fractured into the alleys beyond, the connecting corridors, the narrow spaces before the hull section. The large current was dead. In its place, several small front lines came alive.

Hazran now had to hold not as a whole city
but as the small organs left inside one.

Nasim barely tore his eyes from the central line and turned the people again.

"Don't make the face like it's over!"
He shouted, sharp.
"It's not over. If it were, we wouldn't be running like this!"

The words were close to forced.
But forced was what was needed.
People who've watched something collapse
can have their will go dark along with it if they let their guard down for even a moment.

Luhai heard that voice and moved his feet again, even if by force.
He pushed the two children he'd rescued earlier further into the inner tents,
then ran back out. His face was already a mess of desert dust and sweat,
but his eyes weren't fully dead yet.

Sion found his place again too.

He was no longer the bridge connecting west and inside.
Now he needed to be the hand filling the gaps between the corridors that remained after the break.
Who was lost, not knowing the new lines.
Where things were blocked.
Which corridor could still get people out.

Reading that,
running,
telling people,
pulling them along.

The battlefield had gotten smaller,
but the work had only grown.

Seorin watched him and said, short.

"Don't be late this time."

It sounded like a jab.
It was actually telling him to stay alive and keep running.

Sion gave a short nod instead of answering and turned his body again.

In the inner section,
Jiwoo finally lifted her hand once.

It wasn't completely finished.
But the outside had been pushed to a point where she couldn't stay here holding on any longer.

The joint was trembling low.
The receiving frame was clearly holding better than before.
Aka already knew without touching it—her face said so.

"It will hold."
She said.

Jiwoo let out a rough breath.

"If it doesn't hold, I'll beat it until it does."

That sounded half like irritation,
but even from a distance Sion could tell it was closer to relief.

The ship wasn't dead yet.
On this battlefield, right now,
that meant almost the same thing as the hope of saving a few more people.

Sern stood at the front line again with his side wound roughly tied.

Harun saw it and spoke low.

"Pull back."

Sern shook his head.

"Not yet."

Ater looked at him for a moment.

He didn't stop him.
He only said, brief.

"The next ones will be different."

Sern answered.

"I know."

Both voices were still low,
which made the tension worse.
What would come in from now wouldn't be the same lines as before.
They would be lines that had gone around the collapsed central line and found new paths.

More cautious.
More persistent.
Far more likely to target the inside with precision.

Zahir looked across the entire interior one last time.

Harun at the first line.
Nasim at the human current.
Sion and Seorin in the gaps.
Luhai between information and children.
Kael as the hand temporarily rebinding what had been broken.
Jiwoo and Aka at the hull.
Ater and Sern as the eyes reading the wave.

Hazran didn't have much left.

But still,
each one had a place.

Zahir spoke, very low.

"Good."
He said.
"Now we fight to send them out."

The moment those words fell,
Sion felt something grow heavier once more inside his chest.

Not a fight to hold.
From now on, a fight to send someone from the inside to the outside—
no, beyond Hazran.

Beyond the broken heartline,
Hazran began, like that,
to raise the shape of its final defense again.
