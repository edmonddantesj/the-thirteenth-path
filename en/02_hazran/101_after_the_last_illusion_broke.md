# Episode 101. After the Last Illusion Broke

After Harun went down once,
the battlefield inside Hazran was clearly in the same place
but felt like somewhere entirely different.

The paths were the same.
The hull was still there.
People were still standing in line.

But to Sion,
it felt as if the center of gravity of everything had shifted, just slightly.

That man falls too.

That single truth
changed the air across the entire battlefield.

Now no one could hold, even in the back corner of their minds,
the expectation that *Harun is there, so that side will hold*.

And in the space where that expectation vanished,
a far rawer reality moved in.

Anyone can fall.
And from that moment on,
the remaining people must fill that empty spot with their bodies immediately.

War was probably always like that.
But the people of Hazran
were learning that fact too late and too vividly.

Harun couldn't fully pull back.

Even after his knee folded once,
he braced his hand on the floor and barely raised his body again.
His left arm was more clearly slower now.
His breathing had roughened.
But he didn't go back.

Sern spoke low.

"You truly need to pull back now."

This time,
there was a more compressed tension inside those words.

Harun didn't answer.

But that silence was the answer itself.

Can't pull back.
No—closer to *won't pull back right now*.

Ater said, very brief.

"Then I will go further forward."

Only then did Harun turn his head.

"Don't overdo it."

Those words sounded strangely
more human than anything before.

Not a combat instruction.
More like something said to someone holding together.

Sern cut in immediately.

"All three of us are overdoing it right now."

A short answer.
But right.

Sion looked at the three and understood in that instant:
they were no longer holding by skill alone.
They were holding by pushing back the order of collapse.

Who breaks down less first.
Who leaves one more beat remaining.

That was the true shape of the right corridor front line now.

The hull line behind still couldn't stop.

Nasim's voice had gone past hoarse—it was nearly torn—
but he kept pushing the next person forward.

"Fourth group, attach!"
"Front three, ready!"
"Stop and it's really over!"

That wasn't a threat. It was fact.

Luhai had reached a face that couldn't even cry anymore.
He held a child's hand,
pushed a wounded person's back,
caught an ankle about to trip and set it upright,
speaking only very quietly.

"Eyes forward."
"Go now."
"It's okay—even if it's not, go."

That last line meant *go even though everyone knows it's not okay*,
and it stayed caught in Sion's chest for a long time.

Seorin had grown sharper.

After watching Harun go down,
her hands actually became more precise.
Bracing people upright.
Cutting lines.
Blocking the rear.
Shoving stretchers through.
Each movement stripped of waste, almost like blade-work.

But Sion knew.

That wasn't stability.
She had seen too directly that wavering means dying,
so she was leaving no room to waver at all.

Jiwoo received the fourth group's first three at the hull entrance,
wearing a face as if she'd bitten the inside of her lip.

The ship was still receiving.
But the hold was getting rougher.
Each time one side of the ramp rang hard,
her eyes shook with it.

Aka said very quietly beside her.

"It is getting rougher."

Jiwoo answered, low.

"I know."

"But it is still going."

At those words,
Jiwoo made a face that almost broke into the briefest smile.
She didn't manage to smile.
But the expression was close to the kind
where smiling would have turned into crying.

"Yeah."
She said, low.
"Still going."

Those words, strangely,
sounded like the sentence that described this entire scene.

Still going.
People,
ship,
time.

But that *still* was truly short now.

Zahir swept across everyone, once each.

And spoke again.

"When the fourth group ends,"
he said, low,
"redistribute the remaining hands immediately."

No one heard those words as strange anymore.

The shock had already passed.
What remained was only execution.

Sion felt that fact as more frightening.

Because once people pass the stage of being shocked by cruel decisions,
they have truly entered that world.

Hazran had entered it now too.

The fourth group's first three climbed the ramp.

In the right corridor, Harun, Sern, and Ater
were holding with bare-body calculation instead of broken illusions.
On the left, Kael was still gripping the low barrier.
Before the hull, Nasim, Seorin, Luhai, and Sion
were cutting and sending people faster and more precisely.

And Sion, in the center of it, understood.

What remains after the last illusion breaks
isn't a clean word like courage.

Just people,
each in their own place,
trying not to collapse for one more beat.
