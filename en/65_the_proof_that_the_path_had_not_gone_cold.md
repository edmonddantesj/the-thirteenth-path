# Episode 65. The Proof That the Path Had Not Gone Cold

After Aka said "there's one more thing to see first," the air beside the hull went quiet again.

That silence felt less like hesitation and more like sorting.

What they had just seen was already large enough. A hull that had not died. The ember was not mere decoration but proof that could keep the hull alive. The sense that not any etherite would do.

But Aka did not stop there.

Sion found that strangely more trustworthy. If she had ended it here, it would have felt like another Hazran-style trade. Show something big, say half, tell them to trust the rest. But Aka had never held people that way. She acted like someone who knew that if she did not show everything to the end, people died bigger later.

Aka walked first into a narrow passage behind the hull.

Jiwoo followed without hesitation, and Sion kept close behind, ember gripped in his hand. The passage inside was darker. The faint lighting from the outer work compartment barely reached, and instead a very low vibration continued from somewhere inside the walls. Less a machine sound—closer to the trembling of a structure that had stood still for a long time yet never fully shut down.

Sion felt that vibration was strangely similar to the ember in his hand.

Both small. Both quiet. But neither fully severed.

Aka stopped at the end of the passage.

Before her lay a small room, hollowed in a circle.

Not a workshop. Not a living space. Not a hull storage bay.

It felt more like a station built for watching something for a long time. The walls were almost entirely covered. At the center sat a single low pedestal. On it rested a black metal fragment—neither large nor vivid. At first glance it could have passed for a cooled shard.

But the instant Sion stepped inside, he knew.

The ember in his hand shifted by the smallest degree.

It had not grown. It had not surged.

Instead, very quietly—like before the three etherite fragments—it became less uncomfortable.

As though it already remembered whatever lay before it.

"What is this."
Sion asked low.

Aka did not answer immediately.

Instead, she went close to the black fragment on the pedestal and looked down at it for a long time. Her gaze was less that of someone explaining and more that of someone re-confirming something she had kept for a long while.

"A cooled site."
She said.

Sion did not understand from those words alone.

Jiwoo reacted first.

"A site where fire passed through?"

Aka nodded shortly.

"It didn't die."
She said.
"It just couldn't go all the way."

In that moment, Sion looked down at the ember in his hand without meaning to.

Something that could not go all the way. Not dead, but never opened.

This resembled what they were carrying right now. Not a complete door. Proof that the path had not gone cold.

Aka touched the edge of the pedestal very lightly.

"The paths inside here didn't all die at once."
She said.
"There are places left that started opening and stopped."

Hearing that, Sion felt every phrase he had heard until now—"the thing imitating a door," "the path has not died," "if only the shape matches it dies fast"—all converging here.

It had not vanished completely. Cooled sites remained at intervals. So what was needed now was not building a new path from nothing, but something closer to seeing whether the points that had gone cold could be reconnected.

"Is this what lifts the hull?"
Sion asked.

Aka shook her head.

"No."
She said.
"This is proof that it once departed."

That sentence changed the air inside the room entirely.

Jiwoo's gaze shifted too.

Until now it had been the eyes of someone reading salvageable structure. Now it was closer to eyes trying to read what grammar this hull had actually been built upon—not a mere possibility but a real foundation.

"So,"
She said low.
"What you're holding isn't a guess. It's a remaining trace."

Aka nodded slowly this time.

"Yes."
She said.
"Everyone wanted to say it was gone. But it was never fully gone."

Sion understood why those words struck so hard.

Someone had wanted to erase it. Someone had wanted to believe there was no door. Someone had left only the imitation and rolled it like a stake on the board. But in truth, sites that had not fully cooled remained.

And Aka had known. She had known alone. That was why she had never told just anyone.

Sion asked very low.

"Why show us now."

Aka did not look at Sion directly this time either.

Instead, she looked at the ember in his hand first. Then looked once at Jiwoo before speaking.

"Because now you brought it back."
She said.
"Not pretending to be dead."

A short sentence. But Sion felt that single line was the most precise answer to why Aka had never fully let them inside until now.

Hands that only chased rumors. Hands that only wanted money. Hands that got excited just seeing the imitation of a door—there was no reason to show them this place.

But hands that carried the real thing back alive. Hands that recognized a hull that had not died for what it was. Hands that wanted not to imitate the shape but to see whether it could truly be bridged—only then could it be shown.

Jiwoo took one more step toward the pedestal and said low.

"Then the next step is how to mesh this cooled site with the hull's grammar."

Aka moved the corner of her mouth by the smallest degree this time.

"Yeah."
She said.
"Now we're seeing the same thing."

Hearing that, Sion let out a very small breath.

All through the journey here, it had felt like running across boards that someone else kept laying down. But right now, for the first time, it felt less like standing on someone's board and more like standing before the traces of a path that had actually remained.

And those traces were saying it clearly.

It was never fully over. There had only been hands that made everyone believe it was.
