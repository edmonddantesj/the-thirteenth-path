# Episode 28. What Opens Without a Name

The dying discrimination unit came alive again only for a moment.

When one faint afterglow brushed across the surface of the larger fragment, the metal grain in Kael's hand trembled low. That tremor was not a simple reaction. It was a signal that the things that could be attached here and now truly still remained. Severed records, fragments left behind, field afterglow, and even the people standing close together now without fully trusting one another. All of it was the instant when it was barely about to interlock again into a single line.

Ater drew his breath very slowly.

"Now."

Sion already had his gaze fixed along the thinnest afterglow line still alive inside. The fine grain left on the structure wall was more unstable than before, but it was not completely dead. A line like the final nerve of something that had been not a path, yet a path. That line had to touch the larger fragment in Kael's hand.

Sern said low.

"Half a beat late on the left. If you attach it immediately, it will rebound."

Hearing that, Kael twisted the fragment very slightly. It was not a showy movement. The smallest adjustment only someone who knew weight could make. While he moved, Sion followed the speed at which the afterglow wavered, and Ater was watching from which angle the severed approval line could connect again.

Beyond the close-range channel, Han Jiwoo's voice brushed past shortly.

"Reaction rising."

Seorin cut in at once.

"Finish it in one go."

After that, hands moved before words.

Instead of pushing the larger fragment in toward the slot, Kael first hooked only one edge. Sion understood at once why he had not inserted it directly. If it was pushed in completely, the reaction might die. What they needed now was not insertion but contact. The first meeting that would make the remaining grains recognize one another.

Ater said almost like a breath.

"Good. Like that."

Sern added almost at the same time.

"Now."

Sion reached out briefly along the final afterglow line. His fingertip did not touch directly. He only read the direction of the fine light that flowed over the metal surface and broke, and with his eyes cut out first the width by which Kael needed to move the fragment further.

"Two spaces right."

Kael followed without asking.

At that instant, one pattern that had been dead at the center of the fragment suddenly came alive.

Thin lines spread and connected over the old metal surface, and part of the approval section they had thought cut away recovered its own shape for a very short moment. It was not a complete restoration. But the fact that a severed circuit had breathed again even once was enough.

A low vibration rose from inside the structure.

At first it was distant and small, but soon it became clear enough to travel beneath their feet. It was the tremor that came when a long-dead device began operating again, holding welcome and unease at the same time.

Sern's voice sank sharply.

"The maintenance time is short."

Ater was already reading the pattern lines that had appeared on the fragment.

"These are not coordinates."
He said quietly.
"They are an approval sequence before coordinates."

Sion asked at once.

"In one line."

"This path was hidden not by location, but by passage order."
Ater's gaze did not leave the pattern.
"And that sequence is also what those who erased the names severed first."

A short silence.

It was too important a sentence, so no words attached to it immediately.

What had just appeared was not location but order.

Kael said very low.

"So that is why it did not open by finding only the names."

Instead of answering, Ater read one more line beneath the pattern. At that moment, his eyes changed very slightly.

"This is..."

Sion looked at him.

"What."

Ater said very slowly.

"There is not only one approving subject."

Sern reacted first.

"Multiple approvals?"

"Yes. At least two."
Ater said low.
"One is an Imperial approval line, and the other... I cannot be certain because it is severed, but it is different from the Alliance-side system as well."

Sion's expression hardened.

Kael's fingers trembled faintly while holding the fragment's edge. He was reaching the same conclusion too.

Beyond the close-range channel, Han Jiwoo's voice came low.

"Outside isn't good either. Debris is moving."

Seorin followed immediately.

"If it doesn't finish inside, cut it right away."

No sooner had that warning fallen than somewhere inside the structure, the sound of metal tearing cracked out shortly.

Sion lifted his head reflexively.

The sound came from deeper inside. It was not the simple vibration caused by the structure opening again. It was friction with direction, the kind made when someone passes or when something that had been holding out comes loose. As this discrimination unit came alive now, another part that had been closed also shook with it.

Sern said almost at the same time.

"One more connection section is waking."

Force entered Kael's hand.

"The good kind?"

"I do not know yet."
Sern answered.
"That is why it is worse."

Ater swallowed a short breath.

"The lower pattern is opening further. We have to hold a little longer."

Sion judged at once.

"Kael, maintain."
And to Sern.
"If it collapses, where goes first?"

"The right auxiliary foothold."

Before that answer had even ended, a dull rupture sounded from beneath the metal plate on that side.

Kael turned half his body without releasing the fragment. The movement was so natural that only then did Sion understand with his body why that person had endured all the way here alone. It was less the movement of someone holding a sword, and more the movement of someone who receives with his body the direction that collapses first. When Kael pulled out the metal handle at his waist as if drawing it free, a short blue afterglow skimmed very shallowly along its edge.

He did not swing it.

Instead, he set it briefly against the edge of the collapsing auxiliary foothold as a brace.

The sound of metal striking metal rang out harshly. It did not block it completely, but the speed of the collapse slowed by one beat. Exactly that much was enough. One beat for Ater to read more of the pattern, for Sion to connect more of the afterglow, and for Sern to calculate the next collapse order again.

Sion clenched his teeth.

"So that is a blade."

Kael did not lift his gaze.

"Right now it's a prop."

It was a short answer, but that one line was enough.

He was not someone for whom attack and defense were separate. Someone who used a cutting tool as it was to hold things up too. Someone who opened paths, blocked them, endured them, and cut them if necessary. Only then did it become a little clearer why Kael had entered first and was still alive.

Ater suddenly opened his mouth.

"I saw it."

Everyone's gaze turned that way.

At the lower part of the pattern, in the section that had been dead until moments ago, a very short sentence had appeared. It was not a complete record. But the core remained.

`A name does not prove passage.`

And below it, the next line nearly breaking away.

`Only when the order is correct does the path open.`

Those two lines rearranged this entire site.

A name alone was not enough. What had been severed was not one person, but the very possibility of walking that path again.

Beyond the close-range channel, Seorin said very low.

"Good. Now it finally makes sense why the name alone wasn't enough."

Han Jiwoo added shortly too.

"But I don't think we have time to admire it for long."

He was right.

The sound of metal rupturing burst again beneath the auxiliary foothold Kael was supporting. The brace would not last forever. The two lines and part of the approval line they had secured now might be all.

Sern said firmly.

"We must withdraw."

Ater asked without yet being able to take his eyes off the pattern.

"The record?"

Sion answered immediately.

"We have to live to keep reading."

Kael lifted his eyes briefly at that. This time he did not smile. Instead, only a very faint expression close to acknowledgment passed over his face.

Sion called low.

"Pull the fragment out."

When Kael put force into it, the larger fragment came loose again from the slot edge. The pattern lines weakened at once, and the faint light of the discrimination unit also began dying again. But it was not already too late.

That alone had made this site entirely different from what it had been at the beginning.

"Back."
Sion said shortly.

Sern first marked the order of foot placement that had not collapsed, and Ater repeated once inside his lips like someone trying not to lose the sentence he had read from his mind. Kael left his weight on the auxiliary foothold side until the very end, buying half a beat for the three to get out, and only then pulled his body away.

And in the very next instant, part of the structure that had held until just now collapsed deep downward.

The sound of the fall was long.

Even after that sound stopped, no one spoke immediately.

They all knew.

What they had just opened was not a single door.
It was the first proof that the order severed before the names truly existed.
