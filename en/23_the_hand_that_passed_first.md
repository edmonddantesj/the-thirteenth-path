# Episode 23. The Hand That Passed First

The guidance reaction that had stirred to life inside the structure was weak, and the traces of a recent departure were unmistakable.

If only one of those had been present, the decision would have been easy. If only the guidance reaction remained, they would have pushed deeper inside. If only the recent traces existed, they would have given chase immediately. The problem was that both were here now. The remaining path was not yet dead, and whoever had taken the larger fragment first had not vanished all that long ago.

Sion spoke first.

"Let's decide. Deeper inside first, or bite the trail now."

Sion didn't answer right away. His eyes were still moving between the darkness deeper in the structure and the recent traces outside the slot. The weight-shift marks left by whoever had taken the larger fragment, the finely scratched metal surface, and the trace of someone turning their body sharply as they hurried back out. It was the trail of someone who had fled—and at the same time, the trail of someone who knew how to read this structure.

Sern said in a low voice.

"If we're going to chase, now is when the trail is freshest. Any later and the dust layer and vibrations will kill the traces."

Ater pointed to the other side almost simultaneously.

"But if the remaining reaction deeper inside cuts out, the cost of reopening this entire structure could grow significantly."

Han Jiwoo spoke from the hull side, her voice low.

"Neither of you is wrong. That's what makes it worse."

Sion looked into the inner darkness and said very slowly.

"We can't have both."

"Obviously."
Sion said in a low voice.
"That's why I'm saying let's decide now."

Sern examined the area near the recent departure traces again. The dust layer had been broken once, and fine metal powder was scattered outward. If someone had yanked the object out in panic, their center of gravity would have shifted much more dramatically—but these traces were surprisingly orderly. Hurried, but not collapsed. In other words, someone familiar with the site.

He said very quietly.

"The other party is at least not an amateur. These are the traces of someone who knows how to clean up even while rushing."

Ater looked again at the recent scratch marks. The position from which the larger fragment had been extracted, the angle at which debris had caught and remained, the degree of damage to the slot's edge. It was right. These weren't the traces of someone ignorant of the structure ripping things out in haste. This was a hand that had understood the logic here at least once before moving.

He said quietly.

"Whoever passed through first is someone who can read this structure."

Sion didn't react to that immediately. Not just a thief, but someone who had passed the discrimination and precisely picked out the larger fragment. Then this wasn't a matter of stolen property. It was a matter of who had read this path first.

Over the short-range channel, Seorin's voice came in briefly.

"Then all the more reason to chase now."

Ater raised his gaze.

"On what basis."

"The inside will still be there, but the hand that passed first won't."
Seorin cut cleanly.
"You can re-read a structure. Once you lose a person, they're gone."

A brief silence.

Sion heard that and lifted the corner of his mouth just slightly. Seorin was right. The inner reaction was unstable but still alive. But if a person got any farther away, out here in the outer cluster they would simply dissolve into the void.

He finally raised his head.

"We chase."

Ater didn't press further. That decision might have looked emotional, but in truth it was the most pragmatic choice. And he was uncomfortably, clearly aware that he himself could find no reason to oppose it.

Sern asked immediately.

"Order of advance."

Sion pointed in the direction the recent traces continued.

"Whoever left didn't go straight outside. They hid in the shadow of the inner structure first, then redirected toward the external access strip."

Han Jiwoo picked it up right away.

"Then I'll pull the hull out a bit further to set up a recovery angle."

"No."
Sion shook his head.
"Jiwoo, you hold the hull. If someone comes back around, we need a ship to return to."

Han Jiwoo didn't argue. Instead, over the short-range channel, only a very brief breath came through.

Over the short-range channel, Seorin's voice came in.

"Who takes point."

"Me."
Sion said.
"Sern in the middle. Ater reads the structure from the rear."

After a beat, Seorin said briefly.

"If you push too hard, I cut first."

Sion answered, eyes forward.

"I know."

When the brief exchange ended, the three moved very quickly. Sion turned first along the recent weight-shift marks toward the structure's exterior. The way wasn't laid out like a path. Severed loops and broken footholds, external alignment debris, half-exposed dock-maintenance frames in narrow gaps. This wasn't a corridor—it was a junction that only someone who knew the structure could barely squeeze through. Which meant whoever had passed first also knew how to read this route.

Sern spoke from behind immediately.

"Left foothold—load is dead."

Sion shifted his foot the instant he heard it. The very next moment, the metal plate he'd been about to step on quietly gave way below. Not so much a fall as a silent collapse of something that had been barely holding.

Over the short-range channel, Seorin lowered her breath.

"Would've dropped long ago if you'd come alone."

"If I'd come alone I wouldn't have come at all."

"That's a lie."

Sion didn't respond and looked straight ahead.

On the inner wall of the structure's shadow, a much clearer trace remained this time. A scuff on the metal surface as if someone had grazed their back of hand, and beside it a very short piece of torn fiber. It looked like the remnant of a coat hem or glove lining that had caught and snagged.

Sern reached out and very carefully picked up the fiber.

"Fresh."

Ater's gaze sank the moment he saw it.

"Not Imperial specification."

Sion asked immediately.

"Alliance side?"

Ater wasn't certain.

"At least not Bureau of Endorsement standard. And not port laborer issue either."

That was the most uncomfortable kind. Not Imperial, not Alliance, not a bottom-tier street trader either. A grey hand that had read the structure and entered first from outside any power.

Sion looked longer at the back-of-hand mark on the metal surface beside the fiber than at the fiber itself.

"That wasn't scratched while running. That's a mark left while stopping and bracing."

Ater picked up quietly.

"Then the other party also paused here once to read the direction before moving again."

Sion pointed ahead very slowly.

"That way."

The point where the structure's outer shadow ended, where external access strip debris and old alignment plates overlapped. The most suitable position for someone to hide and then exit again.

The independent objective was now clear. Reading the inner reaction further was also important, but what was more urgent now was not losing the hand that had taken the larger fragment and passed through first. The remaining reaction was weak, and the traces were dying fast. And the only ones who could read this scene and follow from this very spot were them.

The four lowered their breath simultaneously.

And right then, from the darkness ahead came the very faint sound of metal scraping against metal.

As if someone had not yet gotten far.
